So what does "respects the same privacy-overlay discipline" even mean for a surface that by definition displays before any authentication happens? If the honest answer is "the dollar figure just sits there in plain view on the home screen any time someone glances at the phone," that quietly guts the entire app-lock premise this product was built around — the whole reason Face ID got added in the first place was "a list of valuables and what they cost shouldn't be casually visible," and a widget showing a live total value is arguably worse than the in-app screens, because it needs zero interaction to see. Second thing: nobody has actually tested a real backup-and-restore cycle, and that's the one place where I think this build has a documented promise with zero verification anywhere in this whole process. Multiple phases — product_research, per_app_product_brief, ios_architecture_review — all lean on "standard encrypted device backup coverage should be explicitly confirmed and communicated to the user" as the entire safety net given there's no cloud sync. That's not the same claim as "your $40,000 collection survives a phone loss because iCloud/iTunes backup actually captures the SwiftData store and the on-disk photos correctly." I want an explicit step: back up a device with real items/photos/entries on it, wipe or use a second device, restore from that backup, and confirm the catalog, photos, condition/value history, and subscription entitlement all come back intact. Does Catalog Home stay scroll-smooth, does the collection-level export at "many items" (not just many photos on one item) actually stay responsive, does the widget's debounced recompute still feel instant when there's real data to churn through?
