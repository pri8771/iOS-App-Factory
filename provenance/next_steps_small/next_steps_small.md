Claude read that and conceded it straight out rather than defending the original position — but split the export requirement in a way both landed on: the export pipeline has to be structurally real and correct in this slice (right photos, right dates, timelines laid out distinctly, purchase facts present, tested at zero/one/many items), but it doesn't need final museum-catalog typography polish yet — that's a legitimate "do it well later" item, not a "skip it now" item.

On categories, Claude walked back their own suggestion once they thought it through against what was already locked upstream: since all six categories share one schema with no per-category logic differences, narrowing to 2-3 for validation wouldn't actually save any build effort, it would just arbitrarily under-test the cross-category promise that's the whole point of the app. So all six stay in.

On app-lock, this was never really a disagreement — Codex simply hadn't mentioned it either way in round 1, and in round 2 explicitly said it should be in, cheap and trust-critical. Both agree it belongs in the first slice.

One small thing that isn't fully nailed down: Codex said single-item export is mandatory and collection-level export "can wait one step if needed," while Claude wants both in this slice. Nobody pushed back on Claude's stronger version, and since the collection export is a thin wrapper over the same per-item rendering logic once it exists, I'm treating this as settled in favor of including both — it's not a real fork, just Codex hedging slightly more than Claude did.

## Final Output

**MVP slice (the one workflow this build must prove):** A user captures a photo of something they own (any of the six fixed categories — watch/vinyl/card/wine/sneaker/other, Vision silently pre-filling the category suggestion in the background), gives it a title, and saves it in well under a minute. The item appears in an archival-card list. From its detail screen, the user adds one condition/provenance entry (note + optional photo) and one value entry, seeing them laid out as two clearly separate timelines, never merged. The user can soft-delete an item and recover it within the grace window, and the whole catalog sits behind Face ID/passcode app-lock. Finally, the user generates a local PDF export — for that single item, and for the collection as a whole — that is structurally correct (right cover photo, right dated entries, condition and value history laid out distinctly, purchase facts present) even if its final typographic polish isn't fully dialed in yet.

**Must-have interactions:**
- Create an item with only photo + title + category required; save completes in well under a minute.
- All six categories available and functionally identical under the hood (shared schema, free-text subtype).
- Vision classification pre-fills the category chip silently; never blocks or delays save; fully overridable.
- Resume a half-finished capture after interruption (background, force-quit) without losing the photo/draft.
- On the detail screen, independently add: purchase facts, one condition/provenance entry, one value entry — shown as two visually distinct timeline modules.
- A defined near-empty state for the value ledger at 0-1 entries (a simple list/table of entries is enough; no rendered chart needed yet).
- Soft-delete an item and restore it within the recovery window.
- App-lock via Face ID/passcode gating entry to the catalog.
- Generate and preview a PDF export for one item and for the full collection; verified structurally correct at zero items, one sparse item, and one fully-populated item — photos, dates, condition/value history, and purchase facts all render correctly, even with rougher typography than the final design pass.
- Relaunch persistence: everything above survives an app relaunch, not just the current session.

**Cut list:** Widget, StoreKit/paywall, rendered value-over-time chart (a plain list/table of value entries stands in for now), Vision as anything beyond silent background pre-fill, settings/backup-messaging screen, narrowing category count (rejected — all six stay, since they're free once the shared schema exists), multi-photo galleries beyond one photo per timeline entry, final export typography/design polish (deferred, not dropped — the pipeline must be structurally correct now, gallery-ready visuals come later).

The PDF export (single-item and collection-level) opens successfully and is structurally honest at zero, one-sparse, and one-fully-populated item counts — no blank/broken renders, no misattributed photos, no garbled dates. If all of that holds, one complete, trustworthy workflow exists end-to-end, and the next phase can proceed straight to build without reopening product debate.
