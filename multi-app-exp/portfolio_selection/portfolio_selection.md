**Gemini (Design Lead · Systems Thinker) — Round 1**

As the Design Lead and Systems Thinker, my proposal is to build two distinct applications that leverage local-first strengths to solve acute, recurring user pain.

For the first app, targeting the Food & Drink category, I recommend PrepStack. The core user friction is cooking from web-based recipes that are bogged down by ads, trackers, and long preambles. When executing a recipe, users struggle with auto-lock screens, scaling ingredient measurements on the fly, and tracking multiple steps simultaneously. PrepStack will parse recipe text or URLs locally on the device, stripping clutter and generating a clean, high-contrast, step-by-step cook-along interface. It will feature dynamic ingredient scaling, concurrent active timers that display via Live Activities, and hands-free StandBy mode support. Users will pay a subscription for the premium cookbook organization slots and advanced active cooking dashboards because it directly removes daily kitchen frustration.

For the second app, targeting the Travel category, I recommend Wayfarer. Travel itineraries are currently fragmented across email, PDFs, and calendar invites. Accessing details like confirmation codes, hotel addresses, and transit schedules when offline in a foreign airport is a high-stress moment. Wayfarer is a high-density, offline-first travel dashboard. It compiles itinerary items, stores local PDF/image attachments directly within SwiftData, and features an interactive timezone slider showing home, transit, and destination times side-by-side. To handle collaboration without a server, users can export and import entire trips via AirDrop. Users will pay for the reliability of an offline command center, document storage, and the timezone simulator.

The biggest objection to PrepStack is that local parsing of highly unstructured text or diverse website layouts can be brittle without a server-side parser. If the on-device parser misinterprets ingredients, user trust is destroyed. For Wayfarer, the biggest risk is the complexity of timezone calculation across multi-leg flights crossing the International Date Line, and the storage overhead of local document attachments in SwiftData.

The final phase output must preserve the exact SwiftData schema relationships, the schema migration strategy, and a detailed screen inventory specifying the loading, empty, success, and error states for each screen. This will guarantee that the subsequent engineering phases remain focused on delivering a functional, robust codebase rather than generic placeholders.
