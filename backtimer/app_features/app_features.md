Serve-time-first entry. Story: as a host, I set my target serve time before anything else, because it's the anchor everything else is computed from. Acceptance: the app cannot create a dish/step until a serve time exists; changing serve time before Start recomputes every step's target time live.

Dish + flat step list entry (label, duration, active/passive toggle). Story: as a host, I add a dish as a quick list of steps, not a recipe form. Acceptance: adding a step takes three inputs max (label, duration, active/passive) and no ingredient/photo fields exist anywhere in the flow.

Live merged countdown preview after the first dish, re-sorting as more are added. Story: as a host, I see my dishes interleave into one timeline before I've finished entering everything. Acceptance: after saving one step on one dish, a merged timeline view renders; adding a second dish's steps re-sorts that view without a manual refresh or "Generate" tap, in under two minutes for a first-time user with 2 dishes / 4 steps — this is the number the whole concept lives or dies on, so I'd want it instrumented, not just eyeballed once during dev.

Deterministic tie-break for simultaneous step start times (dish entry order as secondary sort key). Story: as a host with two dishes needing action at the same minute, I see a stable, predictable order, not a coin flip. Acceptance: given two steps with identical computed start times, the one from the earlier-added dish always sorts first, verified by a unit test with fixed fixture data, not just visual inspection.

Past-due-step flagging at plan creation. Story: as a host building a plan at 3pm for 6pm serve with a 4-hour marinade, I still get to save the plan, but that step is loudly flagged as already late. Acceptance: a step whose computed start time is before now renders a distinct, unmissable visual state (not just a subtle color shift) and the plan still saves — an all-or-nothing rejection here is a shipped bug, not a design choice, given this was explicitly resolved twice already.

Hard Start confirmation showing the full merged order. Story: as a host, I get one last look at everything before the plan locks and notifications fire for real. Acceptance: Start is gated behind a sheet listing every step in final merged order; dismissing it returns to the builder with zero side effects (no notifications scheduled, no lock applied).

Post-start editing lock, with the forgotten-dish escape hatch or an explicit stated limitation. Story: as a host mid-cook, I can't accidentally corrupt my running notification schedule by editing an in-flight step, but I'm not permanently stuck if I forgot a dish. Acceptance: after Start, every existing step's duration/time fields are non-editable; if we ship the add-a-dish escape hatch, adding a dish post-start merges only into remaining timeline and doesn't touch past steps or serve time — if we don't ship it, the Start confirmation screen must say so in plain text, tested by literally reading the copy in review, because "silence" is not an acceptable outcome here and I don't want it to slip through as a TODO.

Live execution screen: one dominant "next thing to start" plus an always-visible secondary list of other steps currently in progress. Story: as a host with a turkey resting while potatoes mash, I don't lose track of the roast just because something else became the "next" action. Acceptance: with two steps simultaneously in-progress, both appear on screen at once — one dominant, one in a visible secondary list — never fully hidden until its own end-event.

Mark step done early. Story: as a host whose potatoes finished ahead of schedule, I can advance past that step without waiting for its timer. Acceptance: marking a step done early advances execution state immediately and does not recompute any other step's target time — this was already decided as independent, fixed absolute Dates, so it's cheap to build and I don't see a reason to cut it from the full production build even though next_steps_small correctly deferred it from the smallest validation slice.

"Running late" push-back, single control, shifting every not-yet-started step, the serve time, and the end-time of any already-in-progress passive step. Story: as a host falling behind, I hit one button and the whole remaining plan — including the turkey already in the oven — shifts together. Acceptance: triggering the shift with one step mid-flight moves that step's own completion notification, not just future steps; verified with a fixture plan that has one active in-progress passive step at trigger time.

Local notification + Live Activity pairing for every step transition, with force-ended detection on next foreground. Story: as a host with a locked, flour-covered phone, I get told what's next without touching the screen, and if the lock-screen display died, the app tells me the truth on next open instead of pretending nothing happened. Acceptance: backgrounding through a full step delivers both a notification and a Live Activity update with the app never in foreground; manually force-ending the Live Activity (simulator/OS action) and reopening the app surfaces a visible "here's where things actually stand" state, not silent resumption as if nothing happened.

Wall-clock Date-based scheduling surviving force-quit/restart with zero drift. Story: as a host, killing the app by accident an hour into cooking doesn't lose or desync my plan. Acceptance: force-quit mid-countdown, relaunch, and the correct current step displays with the correct remaining time computed from stored Dates, not from an elapsed in-memory timer.

Rolling/windowed notification scheduling to respect the 64-pending-notification ceiling. Story: as a host running an unlimited-dish holiday plan, my 40th step's notification isn't silently dropped because we tried to schedule everything up front. Acceptance: a plan with enough steps to exceed 64 total notifications never has more than the windowed batch (~16-20) pending at once, verified by inspecting `UNUserNotificationCenter` pending requests at multiple points through a long synthetic plan.

Real completion screen. Story: as a host, the payoff moment is an actual screen, not a countdown hitting zero. Acceptance: completing the last step transitions to a distinct completion view; this is trivial to build and I have zero patience for it being cut, since every prior phase already flagged it as the one thing a countdown app most commonly skips.

Permission-denial degraded mode for notifications/Live Activity. Story: as a host who denied one of the two permissions, I'm told my core promise is compromised before I start cooking, not after I miss a step. Acceptance: denying either permission surfaces a visible banner at the Start confirmation screen with a path to Settings; the plan can still be started (not blocked), but the gap is disclosed.

Real paywall/entitlement check with at least one genuine paid unlock (unlimited dishes and unlimited concurrent plans), wired to actual StoreKit, not a stub. Story: as a host who wants more than 3 dishes or a second saved plan, I hit a real purchase flow. Acceptance: attempting to add a 4th dish or start a 2nd concurrent plan on free tier surfaces the paywall; completing a sandbox purchase unlocks both immediately without app restart. I'm putting this in must-have, not should, because "realistic path to monetization" is a hard portfolio requirement and a stubbed tier check that never got upgraded is exactly the kind of thing that quietly ships in a rushed MVP — Apple also requires a working Restore Purchases affordance for any paid entitlement, which I'm folding into this same must-have rather than treating as a separate settings feature.

Accessibility and the four UI states on both surfaces. Story: as any user, VoiceOver reads dish/step entry sensibly and Dynamic Type doesn't break the dominant-step display; both Plan Builder and Live Countdown have real empty, loading, success, and error states. Acceptance: VoiceOver can complete dish entry end to end without sighted assistance; each surface's four states are individually reachable and visually distinct, including the "notification silently failed" error state on Live Countdown that's explicitly been called out as the one most apps skip.

DST and midnight-crossing correctness as an explicit build-verification test, not assumed. Story: as a host running a 6-hour Thanksgiving plan across a DST fall-back weekend, my step times stay correct. Acceptance: a synthetic plan spanning a DST transition (or constructed with a fixed calendar to simulate one) produces the same correct step ordering and durations as an equivalent non-DST plan — this needs an actual test case in the build, because "Date arithmetic is probably fine" is exactly the kind of assumption that's cheap to disprove now and expensive to discover from a user's bug report next November.

**Should-haves** — real value, but the MVP survives without them at first ship and none of them touch the core promise: saved/reusable Dish and Plan templates with duplicate-and-adjust (the actual subscription-retention anchor per the product brief, but it's additive on top of the Dish/Plan separation we're already building, so it can trail the paywall by one release without invalidating "production ready"); a minimal settings screen beyond the paywall (subscription status, notification/Live Activity permission status at a glance, about/privacy text); grayed-out-not-deleted visibility for lapsed-subscriber saved plans; haptic pulses at step transitions as a supplement to notifications for someone standing right next to the phone; an adjustable late-button increment beyond the single fixed +10 minutes.

**Could-haves**, genuinely optional and I wouldn't block a ship on any of them: StandBy-mode display treatment for a charging phone propped up in the kitchen; Siri/Shortcuts "what's next" query support; a slightly richer completion screen (share-sheet-worthy visual polish) even though sharing itself stays out of scope; more than one fixed late-button increment choice.

**Won't-build**, restating and closing off scope that's been asked about across every prior phase so it doesn't quietly resurface as "missing" in review: recipe import/OCR/text parsing; oven/burner/resource-contention modeling or any conflict-detection UI; auto-replanning or re-optimization beyond the manual push-back; Apple Watch app; home-screen widgets; sharing/export functionality; cloud sync (architected for, not built); multi-timezone or travel-aware scheduling; any feature that implies "coordinates" or "manages" resources in copy or UI, since that's a promise this app doesn't keep.
