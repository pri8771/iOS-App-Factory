The competitive wedge is not “another list app.” Campfire wins by combining (1) instant local pass-and-play, (2) robust state discipline so turn/order/scoring never desync during real use, and (3) a visual recap artifact that people actually want to send after a gathering. The growth path stays mass-market because the output is social and portable; niche users can still use it, but the hook is that anyone can show the card in a group chat and challenge the next group to beat a funny answer or score.

What makes someone keep the app instead of deleting it after one party: the free pack has to be good enough that the app earns a spot on the home screen instead of getting deleted the morning after, and the recap card has to be something people actually want in their camera roll — not because it's cute, but because it's a real memory object with specific data from that specific night.
