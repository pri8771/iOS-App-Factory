The single biggest risk here is the one the earlier contract already flagged (non-shaming streak logic, real day-30 depth) but I'd go further: religious content correctness is a trust-destroying failure mode if gotten wrong — a wrong shloka transliteration, a conflated Hindu/Jain practice, or a generically "spiritual" tone that reads as appropriative will burn credibility with exactly the audience this needs to retain. Batch-internal divergence between the two temple apps needs to be a real mechanic difference, not "one is Hindu-skinned, one is Jain-skinned" — e.g., one built around a guided-session/streak model (Headspace-forward, audio-led), the other around a structured curriculum/skill-tree with knowledge checks (Duolingo-forward, text/quiz-led) — same as the PO apps, the shared theme is the convergence risk to actively design against.

Practical implication for the first build: the score has to visibly degrade its confidence/completeness label based on what data is actually present, and the two apps need to diverge in real mechanic terms too — plausible split is one centered on correlation-discovery/trends (the "why did X change" analytical tool) and the other centered on periodic structured risk/wellness scoring with an action plan (the "how am I trending against known risk models" tool) — different enough that a user could plausibly want both.
