I'll flag one thing I'm not fully certain about and would want someone to push on: I've made History a calendar grid partly on a tone argument (dots forgive better than a list of labeled rows), but I could see a reasonable counter-argument that a list is more accessible out of the box — VoiceOver moving through a 7-column grid with mixed states per cell is more design work to get right (each DayCell needs a full accessibility label like "July 3rd, completed" or "July 5th, grace day" rather than relying on visual position) than a list would be. I still think the grid is the right call for the emotional reasons above, but that accessibility labeling work on the grid needs to be taken seriously as real effort in the build estimate, not assumed free because "it's just SwiftUI."
