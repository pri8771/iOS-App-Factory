Acceptance criteria: cold launch on a fresh install shows day 1 as current and days 2-14 as locked; after completing day 1's session, day 2 becomes current on next launch without any manual navigation; the trail's visual state matches persisted data exactly after a force-quit and relaunch, not just after a clean backgrounding.

Second must-have: the three session runners themselves, each with real distinct content, not a shared timer screen with a different label slapped on. Third: the streak and completion engine, unified across trails as already decided, with the specific edge cases from detailed_discussion elevated to acceptance criteria rather than left as prose: a session completed at 11:58pm and one at 12:02am the next day credit two different calendar days, not one; a force-quit mid-session on relaunch either offers resume or discards without marking complete — it must never silently count as done; a device timezone change mid-trail (simulate via a real timezone-jump unit test, not just eyeballing) doesn't double-credit or falsely break the streak, because the calendar-day credit is frozen at completion time.
