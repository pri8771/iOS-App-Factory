- When virality and monetization/usefulness conflict, monetization and usefulness win; viral mechanics are additive, never load-bearing.
- Bonus tech (LLM/AR/ML) is evaluated only after judging the concept without it; if removing it makes the app pointless, the app is a tech demo and doesn't make the final 7.
- When breadth and finish conflict, cut scope, not quality. When cleverness and usefulness conflict, choose usefulness.
- Every phase transition must produce an explicit written artifact; stalled phases force-resolve rather than loop, and the fallback is logged visibly in that app's record.
- The final record for each app must preserve not just the winning decision but the rejection logic — why other candidates lost, why the 7 don't overlap, and what "better than competitors" concretely means for each one.
