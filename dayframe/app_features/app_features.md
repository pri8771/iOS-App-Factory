Acceptance: run it against a day with one short clip (trivial case, must not crash on a single candidate), a day with many clips including at least one corrupted/unsupported file (must exclude the bad one, not fail the whole day), a day that's pet-only with no human face (must still produce a real pick, not the worst-scored clip by default because the scorer under-rewards no-face footage), and a day where every clip is dark/blurry/low-signal (must still produce something rather than silently produce nothing — a low-confidence pick beats an absent day when video did exist).

Story: I open the app and see a month grid where days with a pick show a thumbnail and days without video are just visually blank. Acceptance: a video-less day must never show a placeholder that implies "processing" or "error" — it has to read as "nothing happened here," permanently, not as a pending state, or users will keep waiting for a day that will never get a pick.

Day detail playback, watchable immediately without waiting for month-end, is must-have because it's the activation hook the product brief called load-bearing. Acceptance: tap any day with a pick and it plays within roughly a second, no dependency on the month's reel having been rendered yet.

Swap-to-next-best override is must-have, not optional polish, and I want to be blunt about why: this is the feature that keeps the app from feeling broken when Vision picks wrong, which it will, regularly, on ordinary handheld footage. Story: I disagree with today's pick, I tap swap, I get the next-best candidate for that day. Orphaned temp file cleanup on launch is must-have, small in UI surface but a real requirement — an app whose whole pitch is "hands-off, runs quietly" cannot be the app that slowly eats someone's storage. Acceptance: force-kill the app mid-export and mid-iCloud-fetch, relaunch, verify temp files from the interrupted operation are gone within one launch cycle, not accumulating across multiple interrupted sessions.
