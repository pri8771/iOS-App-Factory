Claude’s correction is right, and I don’t think this is a “style of decomposition” issue anymore, it’s a contract issue. If `tech_specs` froze `FinancialDocument` and `AppSettings` into `services_utilities`, and froze `validateFinancialDocument` plus `recomputePlanningResult` into `polish_resilience`, then moving them back under `data_domain` because they “feel domain-ish” is exactly how we get ownership drift and hidden bugs around the fallback estimate again. That split exists for a reason: the pure engine types and math live in `data_domain`; the persistence-facing document contract, import validation boundary, and the one sanctioned recompute choke point are cross-cutting integrity surfaces. I’m with Claude that those four files need to stay where the frozen contract put them. I’m also with her on granularity. Eight fat tasks sounds tidy until two people both need to touch the same bundle to declare “done,” and then you’ve recreated overlap under a different name. The point of this phase is that workers can start immediately, not negotiate inside a mega-ticket about whether auth, entitlement, and currency formatting are “close enough” to count as one thing.

Where I’d push the group to be stricter is on the actual collaboration mechanics, because that’s the part that still tends to go fuzzy even when the JSON is good. The app lives at `/Users/pchordia/Documents/iOS-App-Factory/irregular-income-planner/`. `primary_ui` owns the `.xcodeproj`, app entry, coordinator wiring, root navigation, all feature screens, and `SharedUI`; nobody else edits those unless a task explicitly says integrator-owned, and right now nothing else needs that exception. `data_domain` owns only the pure planning types plus the two observable stores under `Domain/Stores`; that lane does not touch import/export, auth, or the project file. `services_utilities` owns persistence, auth, entitlement, and formatting contracts plus their concrete implementations and fakes. `polish_resilience` owns validation, the recompute helper, redaction modifier, and every test-only file that proves the other lanes didn’t lie to themselves. Forbidden zones should be hard, not polite suggestions: `data_domain` does not edit `SharedUI` or coordinator code; `primary_ui` does not redefine model shapes or add “just one convenience method” to persistence structs; `services_utilities` does not reach into view state; `polish_resilience` does not “fix” production behavior except in the explicitly assigned helper/validator/modifier files. Anything that touches the coordinator, the project file, root DI, or target membership gets announced before edit and merged only after its own acceptance criteria pass locally, because those are the choke points where one lane can silently break three others. Testing responsibility should follow ownership for first-pass verification, but sign-off on integrity crossings belongs to `polish_resilience`: `T-005`, `T-016`, `T-035`, and `T-036` are the anti-self-certification backstops.
