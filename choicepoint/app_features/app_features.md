Captures your reasoning and prediction at the moment of a real decision, then reminds you to check whether you were right.

People making recurring, consequential personal or professional decisions who want to improve their judgment over time.

Making an important decision, forgetting the reasoning within weeks, and never learning if it was actually a good call.

A judgment-improvement tool, not a habit tracker: timestamped decision-and-prediction capture paired with a scheduled future revisit that surfaces your own past reasoning for calibration.

Unlimited decisions tracked, calibration analytics (how often predictions were right), custom revisit schedules, export for professional reflection.

Free: 5 active decisions tracked. Paid: unlimited plus analytics and export.

Fully on-device decision log with local-notification-based revisit prompts.

Optional cloud sync/backup; potential shared decision journals for co-founders or partners deciding together.

## AR / Local ML / Local LLM Fit

Optional on-device LLM to structure a rambling voice note into a clean decision record.

## Design Direction

Calm, restrained, ledger-like typography with no gamification or badges — feels serious and premium.

## App Store Positioning

A journal for your decisions — not your habits. See if your judgment actually improves.

If revisit completion stays low after onboarding tuning, drop the calibration claim and pivot to pure decision-logging.

## v1 Build Scope

Decision capture, revisit scheduling/notifications, outcome logging, basic calibration stats.

### Round 1


**Codex (Design Lead · Systems Thinker) — Round 1**

The feature set needs to stay brutally aligned to one loop: capture a consequential decision fast, bring it back at the right time, force an honest resolution, and turn that into trustworthy calibration. If a feature doesn’t strengthen one of those steps, it is noise in this app.

My must-have set is smaller than the raw spec might tempt us into, because this product dies from friction faster than it dies from missing polish.

- Must: fast decision capture. User story: as someone in the middle of a real decision, I can save a decision in seconds with only the minimum required fields, then enrich it later if I care. Acceptance criteria: a new decision can be saved with decision text, prediction, and revisit date only; context, options considered, confidence, and “what would change my mind” are visible but optional; the save path enforces the 5-active free cap through shared validation; if the user is over cap, the form explains why save is blocked and what counts as active.
- Must: active decision ledger with clear due/overdue organization. User story: as a user with multiple open decisions, I can immediately see what needs attention and what is still pending. Acceptance criteria: the main list distinguishes upcoming, due, and overdue active decisions; overdue items stay visible until resolved or intentionally rescheduled; resolved decisions do not appear as active; empty states are real and specific, not placeholder filler.
- Must: real revisit scheduling via local notifications. User story: as a user who saved a decision, I get reminded at the intended time even if I have not opened the app recently. Acceptance criteria: saving an active decision schedules a local notification according to the chosen revisit date semantics; notification permission denied or revoked is surfaced in-app with a clear fallback state; notification scheduling uses the rolling-window policy so paid unlimited decisions do not silently break at OS limits.
- Must: deep-linked revisit flow that shows the original reasoning first. User story: as a user returning from a reminder, I land directly on the exact decision and see what I originally predicted before logging the outcome. Acceptance criteria: tapping a notification opens the specific decision’s revisit screen, not a generic list; the revisit view displays original decision text, prediction, and any stored reasoning before outcome actions; if the decision was already resolved, the deep link opens a read-only resolved view instead of a broken form.
- Must: structured outcome logging with honest ambiguity handling. User story: as a user revisiting a decision, I can record what happened without being forced into false certainty. Acceptance criteria: the revisit flow captures both narrative outcome text and one structured result of held, missed, or mixed/unclear; “not yet” is a separate action that reschedules without resolving; held/missed/mixed resolve the decision and free a free-tier slot immediately; mixed counts as its own calibration bucket, not as a disguised snooze.
- Must: basic calibration stats from real resolved data. User story: as a user who has completed revisits, I can see whether my predictions are holding up over time. Acceptance criteria: the stats view computes from resolved decisions only; it shows raw counts alongside any percentages; it has a truthful empty state when there is no resolved data; mixed outcomes are represented explicitly rather than collapsed into binary accuracy.
- Must: shared business rules across app surfaces. User story: as a user capturing decisions from the app or Siri, I get the same rules and data integrity everywhere. Acceptance criteria: the in-app capture flow and App Intent path use the same validation, persistence, and cap enforcement; a sixth active decision cannot be created through Siri if it is blocked in-app; any saved decision appears in the same ledger and follows the same revisit scheduling logic.
- Must: restrained privacy-first notification and widget behavior. User story: as a user storing sensitive decisions, I can use reminders and glanceable surfaces without exposing the content publicly. Acceptance criteria: notifications and widgets show due-ness/count only, never decision text; full content is visible only inside the app; there is no v1 setting that weakens this default.

The should-have set is valuable and still plausibly fits the MVP if the core loop is already solid.

- Should: upcoming revisits widget that surfaces counts and nearest due date without exposing content.
- Should: App Intent/Siri quick capture for the minimum-field path only, not a full rich-entry experience.
- Should: lightweight confidence capture in the main form and edit flow, because later calibration gets more valuable if the field exists from day one.
- Should: simple custom revisit presets like 30/60/90 days plus exact date selection, so common decisions are faster to log.
- Should: a permission-status card or settings state that clearly explains when reminders are disabled and how to fix them.
- Should: downgrade-safe subscription gating, where users above the free cap can still view, edit, revisit, and resolve existing decisions.

The could-have set is defensible only if nothing in the must/should layer is compromised.

- Could: richer paid analytics like confidence-band accuracy or simple category breakdowns.
- Could: export of resolved decisions and calibration summaries for coaching or professional review.
- Could: reusable revisit templates for recurring decision types like hiring, vendor selection, or product bets.
- Could: a short post-resolution note field for “what I’d do differently next time,” as long as it stays optional and does not turn the app into journaling.

The won’t-build list matters just as much, because this concept is unusually easy to dilute.

- Won’t build cloud sync, accounts, or collaboration in v1.
- Won’t build a general journal, notes app, or task manager around the decision log.
- Won’t build streaks, badges, scores, celebratory animations, or any other habit-tracker mechanic.
- Won’t build public sharing, social comparison, or community prediction features.
- Won’t build rich tagging, folders, dashboards, or taxonomy-heavy organization that slows capture.
- Won’t build voice-note-to-LLM structuring in MVP.
- Won’t build lock-screen or widget content that reveals the actual decision text.

If I apply MoSCoW with the product promise in mind, the true priority order is capture, revisit delivery, deep-linked confrontation, structured resolution, and only then stats and satellites. The widget and Siri path are in scope, but they are clearly below reliability of the main loop. If a late tradeoff appears, I would simplify those surfaces to the bare minimum before I let them destabilize capture, notification scheduling, or outcome logging.

The biggest risk in my own direction is that even this trimmed must-have list still has two “platform integration” requirements that can eat schedule fast: notifications done properly and App Intent parity with shared validation. That is exactly why I am arguing against richer paid analytics, export polish, and fancy organization in this phase. The build can complete this list, but only if we treat the serious-looking calm UI as a container for a very small number of extremely reliable behaviors.
