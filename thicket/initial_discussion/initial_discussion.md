Thicket should be defined in one sentence as: a local-first iPhone provenance ledger for plant propagators that helps them capture, trace, and prove where every cutting came from, what happened to it, and who it moved to.

The primary user is not a general plant owner. It is a serious hobbyist collector or small-scale trader who propagates from mother plants, swaps cuttings with specific people, and currently loses lineage across Notes, Photos, texts, and memory. The core loop should be brutally simple. Start from a mother plant or received specimen, create one or more child cuttings, log the propagation event with minimal required detail, record custody changes when a cutting is given or received, then later browse that specimen either as a lineage path or as an event timeline and export the record when needed. The app wins if capture is fast in the moment and reconstruction is trustworthy later.

The hard scope boundary needs to stay severe. This is not a plant care app. No watering reminders, no care schedules, no pest tracker, no diagnosis, no plant ID gimmick, no social feed, no marketplace, no shipping suite, no gardening reference library. If a feature does not improve provenance legibility or reduce capture friction, it should be out. The required in-scope domain is source plants, derived cuttings, propagation events, transfer or custody events, counterparties, photos, notes, and lightweight tags or collections. Parent-child lineage tracking cannot be paywalled because that is the product’s reason to exist.

The measurable success criteria should be concrete.
