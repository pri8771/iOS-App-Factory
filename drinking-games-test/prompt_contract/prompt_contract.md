## Original Prompt

Build Kings — a multiplayer drinking game app for iOS (local pass-and-play).

Core mechanics:
- 2–6 players draw cards from a deck in turn order

"Made a rule" (Joker) is a real functional hazard, not just a rules quirk. In real Kings, Joker rules persist and stack for the rest of the game and can override other card behavior (e.g., "no pointing," "always say 'I' before your name"). If this app treats Joker literally — free-text rule creation that has to somehow be "enforced" across future turns — that's arbitrary user-generated text with no mechanical effect, i.e., decorative only. That's fine, but the contract needs to say explicitly that custom Joker rules are flavor text with zero mechanical enforcement, displayed as a reminder banner at most, so nobody later tries to build a rule-parsing engine for free-form English.

End conditions are underspecified and contradictory as written. "Cup is empty" (a King's Cup mechanic where drinks/dares get added to a communal cup, presumably by K draws) vs. "10 turns pass" — turns or full deck? A 6-player game hits 10 turns in under 2 rounds; a 2-player game may cycle through most of a 54-card deck in 10 turns and never trigger the King's Cup at all if Kings are rare early draws. I want "10 turns" clarified as "10 total card draws across all players" (not 10 rounds), and the cup-empty condition defined precisely: does the cup start "full" and drain, or start empty and fill until some threshold, and what actually triggers game-end — last of the 4 Kings drawn, or a numeric cup-fill counter?
