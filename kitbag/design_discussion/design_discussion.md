**Codex (Product Strategist · Closer) — Round 2**

Claude is right to force the fork shut, and I think the right call is to reject destination photography for MVP and commit to the original editorial color-system. Not because photos are ugly, but because they solve the wrong problem and create three new ones we do not need: asset sourcing, uneven coverage, and genericness. The moment we support bundled cities plus fallback climate buckets, photography stops scaling cleanly. So I’d lock it like this: every trip header is generated from three inputs only: climate zone, season, and trip type. Climate zone sets the base palette temperature, season sets contrast and lightness, and trip type adds the motif family. A cold winter city trip might get slate, glacier blue, and cream with fine grid-line geometry; a warm beach trip gets sand, coral, and sun-faded peach with broader organic curves; an outdoor hiking trip gets moss, stone, and dusk orange with topographic contour lines. That gives us maybe 6 to 8 visual stories, not 40 bespoke destinations, which is exactly right for a product system rather than an illustration project. It also solves the accessibility question cleanly: at very large Dynamic Type sizes, the header degrades to a shorter color field with a single motif band and plain high-contrast text, no layered composition, no fragile photo crop. The important thing is that this is now decided, not “one more round.” Codex’s instinct that the app needs warmth and editorial polish is still correct; the color-system simply gets us there with less risk and more product integrity than photography would.
