Second, unit and quantity math is a landmine. Materials get purchased in one unit (a 5lb bag of flour, a spool of thread) and consumed per-batch in another (grams, inches). Either is fine, but it can't be quietly glossed over, because a wrong number in an accounting app isn't a cosmetic bug, it's the entire product failing at its stated job.

I'd bank the batch's cost inputs at creation time; recompute-on-the-fly would make "profit trend" meaningless since you couldn't tell if a trend reflects real improvement or a retroactive price update.

Production-readiness for this phase means: builds and runs on a real simulator/device target with no crashes across the five core workflows and the paywall flow; every screen reachable in normal use has defined empty/loading/success/error states, including the two named ones; SwiftData model can survive app relaunch and a reasonably-sized dataset (dozens of products, dozens of batches each) without degrading; VoiceOver can complete every core workflow, not just have labels; StoreKit 2 entitlement gating is enforced, not just visually implied; and there's an explicit, written decision (even if the decision is "not supported in v1, and this is a disclosed limitation") for the unit-conversion and cost-snapshot questions above.
